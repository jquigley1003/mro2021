Most aircraft owners will go through the process of repairing or overhauling their aircraft engines 2 or 3 times in their lifetime, while FBO Directors of Maintenance may process 50 engine overhauls in their career. Large engine maintenance, repair, and overhaul companies process hundreds of major engine repairs and overhauls each year with focus on shop flow efficiency, maximizing their profitability, and not building an engine that meets customer needs at the most economical price. The turbine engine repair and overhaul processes are complex and continue to grow in complexity as companies seek to increase throughput, reduce overhead and maximize their profitability by outsourcing engine accessory and component overhaul and outsourcing warranty, billing, and other administrative functions to third party companies.

Technical and academic, I developed technical expertise by serving as a Jet Engine Technician for 10 years in the United States Air Force & Air Force Reserve working on Pratt and Whitney JT3, JT8 and F100 engines at both on-wing repair and overhaul maintenance levels. I received and have held an FAA Aircraft & Powerplant license since 1991. I received an associate’s degree in Airport Management and bachelor’s degrees in both Air Science and Aeronautical Engineering from Embry Riddle Aeronautical University’s Daytona Beach Campus prior to beginning my career specializing in aftermarket repair and overhaul of Pratt & Whitney Canada Engines in 1994.

My corporate career, working in Pratt & Whitney Canada engine repair and overhaul management began in 1994 and has spanned almost 30 years. Starting as a Customer Service Manager at Airwork Miami, progressing to Program Manager at National Airmotive Corp./Rolls Royce, and becoming Vice President at ATech Turbine Components prior to Pratt and Whitney Canada recruiting me in 2002. In October 2020 I completed 18 years in technical sales as a Regional Sales Manager for Pratt & Whitney Canada and formed MRO Management Services.

In this nearly three decades, I have overseen and managed more than 4,000 Repairs and Overhauls of P&WC engines while working for manufacturer authorized, independent, and engine manufacturer maintenance facilities. Throughout my career I continuously challenged internal corporate policies and agendas serving as an advocate for customers from as large as FedEx on their Cessna Caravan and ATR fleets, to single Turboprop aircraft owners. I worked at two of Pratt & Whitney Canada’s competitors and thoroughly understand the processes and industry. I am now working independently and formed MRO Management Services to represent owners, operators, and FBO’s directly as your “Turbine Engine Advocate.”
